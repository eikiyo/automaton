# Guidance: Discussion, Limitations & Contribution (D14)
## Gates: D14-01 through D14-09 | Critical: D14-01

### What JIBS Demands
- D14-01 (CRITICAL): Conclusion stays within what results support — no overclaiming.
- D14-02: Discussion interprets through theoretical framework.
- D14-03: Surprising/null results given theoretical explanation.
- D14-04: Theoretical contribution restated concretely in conclusion.
- D14-05: Managerial/policy implications concrete, not generic.
- D14-06: Limitations acknowledged with specificity and direction of bias.
- D14-07: Future research specific and actionable.
- D14-08: JIBS relevance explicit — engages with international dimension.
- D14-09: Abstract complete (motivation, gap, method, finding, contribution) in ≤200 words.

### How to Pass (from ARA Writer & Critic phases)

**Discussion — 5-Beat Structure:**

Beat 1 — SUMMARY (1 paragraph): Restate key findings. Focus on the META-INSIGHT, not repetition.

Beat 2 — THEORETICAL IMPLICATIONS (2-3 paragraphs):
- "This extends [Theory X] by showing [new boundary condition]"
- "This challenges [Author]'s assumption that [assumption]"
- "This integrates [Framework A] and [Framework B], revealing [insight]"
Compare with existing literature — agreements AND disagreements.

Beat 3 — PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS (1-2 paragraphs):
Name the actor → the action → the expected outcome.
NOT: "Managers should consider the implications of..."
YES: "Lending platform operators should implement [specific action] to address [specific problem], which our findings suggest would [specific outcome]."

Beat 4 — LIMITATIONS (1-2 paragraphs):
At least 4 limitations. Frame each as: Limitation → Impact on findings → How future research could address it. Be honest — honest limitations increase credibility.

Beat 5 — FUTURE RESEARCH (1-2 paragraphs):
2-3 specific research questions concrete enough that a reader could design a study from the description.

**Abstract (D14-09):**
Structure: Background (2 sentences) → Gap (1 sentence) → Method (1-2 sentences) → Key findings (2-3 sentences) → Implications (1-2 sentences).
HARD CAP: 200 words. Write this LAST, after all other sections.
Rules: No citations. Must contain research question, method, central finding, primary implication.

**Conclusion:**
- Restate research question and answer in one sentence.
- 3 key takeaways (theoretical, practical, methodological).
- Closing sentence connecting back to the opening hook.
- NO new information. NO new citations unless absolutely necessary.

**STYLE VIOLATIONS — AUTO-REJECTED:**
1. Em/en-dashes: ZERO anywhere. Use commas, semicolons, colons, parentheses.
2. Hollow topic sentences: Never "This section discusses X." Just make the argument.
3. Shopping-list writing: Never 4+ sentences starting with "Author (Year) found..."
4. Bullet lists in body: Top journals require continuous prose.
5. LLM artifacts: No "[INSERT]," "[TODO]," emoji, HTML tags, self-reference as AI.

**Style Guide:**
- Tense: Past for methods/results. Present for theory/discussion.
- Voice: Active preferred. Passive acceptable for methods.
- Person: "This paper" or "we" — never "I."
- Paragraphs: 4-8 sentences each. No single-sentence paragraphs.
- Topic sentences: Every paragraph starts with a claim or argument point.
