# Guidance: Construct Definition & Conceptual Clarity (D05)
## Gates: D05-01 through D05-07 | Critical: D05-01, D05-02

### What JIBS Demands
- D05-01 (CRITICAL): All key constructs formally defined at first use.
- D05-02 (CRITICAL): Construct definitions stable — no drift between theory, hypotheses, and measurement.
- D05-03: Boundaries between similar constructs explicit (e.g., legitimacy vs. reputation).
- D05-04: DV defined conceptually AND operationally, consistently.
- D05-05: Mediators and moderators conceptually distinct from main effect variables.
- D05-06: Every non-standard term defined — no borrowed jargon without grounding.
- D05-07: Level of abstraction consistent (no mixing firm-level constructs with country-level operationalizations).

### How to Pass

**Define Every Construct (D05-01):**
On first mention of any theoretical construct, provide:
- Conceptual definition (what it means theoretically).
- Source of definition (who defined it).
- How it differs from related concepts.

Example: "Institutional voids — defined as the absence of specialized intermediaries, regulatory systems, and contract-enforcement mechanisms that support efficient market transactions (Khanna & Palepu, 2010) — differ from institutional distance (Kostova, 1999) in that they describe absolute absence rather than relative difference between home and host environments."

**Construct Consistency (D05-02):**
Create a construct table early:
| Term | Definition | Source | Section Used |
Use the EXACT same term throughout. Never switch between "institutional voids," "institutional gaps," and "institutional weaknesses" for the same concept.

**Level of Analysis (D05-07):**
If your theory operates at firm level, your constructs must be measured at firm level. If you use country-level data (WGI, Hofstede) as a moderator, justify the cross-level inference explicitly.
