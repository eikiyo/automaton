# Guidance: Causal Identification (D11)
## Gates: D11-01 through D11-07 | Critical: D11-01

### What JIBS Demands
- D11-01 (CRITICAL): Causal claims proportionate to identification strategy — no causal language in purely observational design.
- D11-02: IV instrument relevance and exclusion restriction defended.
- D11-03: DiD parallel pre-trends tested.
- D11-04: RDD manipulation tested.
- D11-05: Reverse causality considered.
- D11-06: Omitted variable threats named specifically.
- D11-07: Placebo or falsification test performed.

### How to Pass (from ARA Synthesis phase)

**Confidence Language Rules (MANDATORY):**
Match language strength to GRADE certainty:
- High: "The evidence demonstrates...", "Research consistently shows..."
- Moderate: "The evidence suggests...", "Findings indicate..."
- Low: "Preliminary evidence suggests...", "Limited research indicates..."
- Very Low: "Very limited evidence tentatively suggests..."

Match language to study count:
- 5+ concordant: "The evidence consistently demonstrates..."
- 3-4 studies: "Several studies suggest..."
- 1-2 studies: "One study by [Author] (Year) found..." — NEVER present as established fact.
- Single study: Use "preliminary", "initial", "one study reported."

**NEVER overstate conclusions.** If you only have 10-15 observational studies, you CANNOT claim "robust evidence." Use "emerging evidence suggests..."

**Overclaiming Detection:**
Flag and fix if you write:
- "This proves that..." (nothing in a review proves).
- "This is the first study to..." (verify — is it?).
- "All evidence suggests..." (is it ALL?).
- "This definitively shows..." (no hedging = overclaiming).
- Strong causal language without experimental evidence.
- Universal claims from limited samples/geographies.
