# Guidance: Research Question & IB Contribution (D01)
## Gates: D01-01 through D01-08 | Critical: D01-01, D01-02, D01-03

### What JIBS Demands
- D01-01 (CRITICAL): A single, precisely scoped research question — not a topic, not a theme.
- D01-02 (CRITICAL): Genuinely international — would collapse in a single-country setting.
- D01-03 (CRITICAL): Theoretical contribution stated explicitly — what do we now know that we didn't?
- D01-04: Empirical contribution distinct from theoretical, stated separately.
- D01-05: Non-incremental — challenges, extends, or reconciles prior theory.
- D01-06: Empirical setting justified for the theory, not data convenience.
- D01-07: Consequence of knowing vs. not knowing stated.
- D01-08: Unit of analysis defined and constant throughout.

### How to Pass (from ARA Hypothesis & Synthesis phases)

**Formulating the Research Question:**
Use the Four Evidence Maps before choosing a question:
1. KNOWLEDGE MAP — What is established (top 8-12 findings).
2. ASSUMPTION MAP — What is believed but untested (at least 5).
3. CONTRADICTION MAP — What is disputed (at least 3).
4. ABSENCE MAP — What is never discussed (populations, geographies, methods, theories).

Your question MUST come from one of these maps, not from intuition.

**Novelty Framework (pick one):**
- INVERSION: Flip the dominant assumption (field assumes X→Y, propose Y→X).
- MISSING_LINK: Unstudied step in a causal chain (A→B→?→D).
- MODERATOR: Hidden boundary condition that splits evidence.
- CROSS_DOMAIN: Import a framework from another field.
- MEASUREMENT: Show the field's standard metric is flawed.
- TAXONOMY: Split a conflated term into distinct sub-phenomena.

**The Five Questions Test (all must pass):**
1. Q1 — Falsifiability: What would make this wrong? Must be specific and testable.
2. Q2 — Positioning: Name specific papers/experts this challenges.
3. Q3 — Mechanism: Concrete causal pathway in ≤2 sentences.
4. Q4 — Weakest point: Identify a real weakness, not a dodge.
5. Q5 — So what: Who changes behavior if this is true?

**IB Relevance (D01-02):**
The question must require cross-national variation to be meaningful. Ask: "Would this question make sense if I removed all international elements?" If yes, it fails D01-02.

**Contribution Statement (D01-03, D01-05):**
BAD: "This review provides a comprehensive overview."
GOOD: "This review is the first to systematically apply [FRAMEWORK] to [TOPIC], revealing that [FINDING] — a pattern obscured by prior reviews' failure to distinguish [X from Y]."
