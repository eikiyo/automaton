# Guidance: IB Theory & Causal Logic (D02)
## Gates: D02-01 through D02-10 | Critical: D02-01, D02-02, D02-03

### What JIBS Demands
- D02-01 (CRITICAL): Formal theoretical framework identified and APPLIED — not merely cited.
- D02-02 (CRITICAL): Causal mechanism explicit: X → Y via Z — not just a predicted association.
- D02-03 (CRITICAL): Theory grounded in IB/management scholarship (OLI, institutional theory, RBV, TCE, agency, signaling, social capital) or application to IB justified.
- D02-04: Chosen theory is strongest available — alternatives considered and dismissed.
- D02-05: Boundary conditions acknowledged — where does it NOT apply?
- D02-06: Multiple theories integrated, not listed independently.
- D02-07: Theory travels to the cross-national context.
- D02-08: Theoretical contribution separated from empirical contribution.
- D02-09: Competing theoretical predictions acknowledged.
- D02-10: Theory explains variance, not just describes a pattern.

### How to Pass (from ARA Synthesis & Advisory Board phases)

**Thesis Selection (D02-01, D02-02):**
Choose ONE thesis type:
- Resolution: "Evidence resolves this debate in favor of X, under conditions Y, because mechanism Z."
- Moderator: "Conflicting evidence on X is explained by moderator M."
- Framework: "Existing approaches conflate A and B, which are distinct phenomena."
- Mechanism: "The X→Y relationship operates through previously unidentified mechanism M."

State your thesis in ONE sentence. This drives every decision.

**Causal Model (D02-02):**
Build an explicit causal chain:
```
Exposure → Mediator A → Outcome
         → Mediator B → Outcome
Confounder 1 → Exposure + Outcome
```
Name each link and cite the studies supporting it. Include:
- Forward causation evidence (longitudinal, experimental, dose-response).
- Reverse causation evidence.
- Confounder analysis table.
- Natural experiments or quasi-causal evidence.

**Theory Grounding (D02-03):**
Acceptable IB theories: OLI paradigm, institutional theory (North, Scott), RBV/VRIO, TCE (Williamson), agency theory, signaling theory, social capital theory, Uppsala model, born-global theory, springboard theory, LLL framework.

For each theory used:
1. Define core constructs.
2. Explain why appropriate for this context.
3. Identify where theories conflict (if multiple).

**Boundary Conditions (D02-05):**
Every framework needs limits. State explicitly:
- Geographic scope (where it applies and doesn't).
- Industry scope.
- Temporal scope.
- Firm size/type scope.

**Advisory Board Check (from ARA):**
The Methodologist asks: "Can the evidence support the claims?"
The Strategist asks: "Will this survive peer review at Q1/Q2?"
Both must approve before writing begins.
